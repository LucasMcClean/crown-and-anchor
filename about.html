About Crown & Anchor

Crown and Anchor is a traditional British gambling game that dates back to the 18th century. The game is typically played with six-sided dice and a special board marked with symbols representing various outcomes. Its origins can be traced to sailors and maritime culture, where it was often played aboard ships or in portside taverns.

The game involves players placing bets on different symbols, such as crowns, anchors, hearts, diamonds, spades, and clubs, which are depicted on the board. The dice are then rolled, and the outcome determines the winners and losers based on the symbols rolled.

Crown and Anchor gained popularity not only among sailors but also in various social circles, becoming a common feature in fairs and festivals. It was widely played in the United Kingdom and its colonies, including Canada and Australia.

Over the years, the popularity of Crown and Anchor has waned, but it still persists as a nostalgic and traditional game in some regions. While not as widespread as it once was, the game remains a part of British cultural history and serves as a reminder of the social and recreational activities enjoyed by communities in the past.

How to Play

Place Your Bets: Place your bets on one or more of the symbols on the board. Each symbol represents a potential outcome of the dice roll. You can bet on as many symbols as you'd like.

Roll the Dice: The three dice are rolled and the outcomes of the dice determine whether you win or lose your bet.

Determine Winnings: You win if the symbols you bet on match any of the symbols rolled on the dice. The payout varies depending on the number of occurrences of the chosen symbol(s). If one die shows the chosen symbol, you'll receive even money (1:1); if two dice show the symbol, your payout is 2:1, and if all three dice show the symbol, your payout is 3:1.

Collect Winnings: After the dice are rolled and the outcomes are determined, you'll collect your winnings if you bet on the winning symbols. Otherwise, you'll lose your bets.
